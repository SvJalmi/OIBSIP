Marie Curie

1867 – 1934

"Nothing in life is to be feared, it is only to be understood. Now is the time to understand more, so that we may fear less."

Biography

Marie Curie was a Polish-born physicist and chemist, famous for her pioneering research on radioactivity. She was the first woman to win a Nobel Prize and the only person to win Nobel Prizes in two different scientific fields—Physics and Chemistry.

Curie discovered the elements polonium and radium, and she developed techniques for isolating radioactive isotopes. She also conducted important work during World War I, developing mobile radiography units to treat wounded soldiers. Her legacy inspires scientists around the world.
